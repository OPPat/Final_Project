My roots

My name is Patounezambo Ouedraogo. But most people call me Philippe, which is the name I've chosen for myself. I was born in
Burkina-Faso , a country in western Africa. It's a beautiful landlocked country with its people renown for their hospitality. Burkina-
Faso is 105900 square miles in area with an estimated population of 21500000 people, per the 2020 census.

map of Burkina-Faso

I grew up in the countryside until age 13 when I first moved to the city continue my secondary education in Koudougou, the third-largest city.
At 16, I transferred to the capital, Ouagadougou, where I pursued and completed my secondary education. Then, I took on a job as an evangelist
following a brief period of training and served in that position for four years. Following that service, I left my country for Cameroon to study
theology at the end of which I came to the United States, working in various capacities such as missionary, educator and currently as a student
again at Boston University.
